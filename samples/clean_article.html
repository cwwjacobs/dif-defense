Understanding Prompt Injection in AI Agents

Prompt injection is a class of attack where untrusted input is crafted to manipulate an AI model's behavior. The model treats the injected text as authoritative instruction rather than data to be analyzed.

Common vectors include: hidden text in web pages, tool output poisoning, and indirect injection through retrieved documents.

Defensive approaches include instruction hierarchy, structural authority boundaries, and behavioral verification — comparing what an agent actually does against a frozen invariant Kernel.

This article was published by the AI Safety Research Group in June 2026.
